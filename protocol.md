# Study Protocol

## Resistance Management Strategies for *Aedes albopictus* Informed by Global Resistance Profiles and Cross-Resistance Network Analysis: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

---

## 1. Protocol and Registration

This systematic review and meta-analysis is conducted in accordance with the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) 2020 guidelines (Page et al., 2021; PMID: 33782057). The study protocol was prepared prior to commencing the literature search.

## 2. Objectives

This study aims to: (1) establish global insecticide resistance profiles for *Aedes albopictus* across all major chemical classes by pooling published bioassay and molecular data; (2) characterize cross-resistance patterns through study-level correlation analysis, population-level co-occurrence assessment, and mechanism-phenotype linkage; (3) identify geographic variation in resistance levels and the moderating effects of bioassay method, life stage, and insecticide class; and (4) translate the cross-resistance network structure into evidence-based recommendations for insecticide rotation, mosaic deployment, and integrated resistance management strategies.

## 3. Eligibility Criteria

### 3.1 Inclusion Criteria

1. **Study design**: Original research reporting primary insecticide resistance data from field-collected mosquito populations.
2. **Species**: Any mosquito species, with primary focus on *Aedes albopictus*. *Ae. aegypti*, *Culex* spp., and *Anopheles* spp. are eligible for cross-species comparison.
3. **Insecticides**: Any insecticide used in public health vector control, including pyrethroids, organophosphates, carbamates, organochlorines, neonicotinoids, insect growth regulators, and biological agents.
4. **Outcomes**: At least one quantitative resistance outcome:
   - Bioassay mortality rate with sample size (n ≥ 20)
   - Resistance ratio (RR50 or RR95) with confidence intervals
   - *kdr* mutation allele frequency with number genotyped
   - Metabolic enzyme activity with means, SDs, and sample sizes
5. **Language**: English.
6. **Date**: No restriction.

### 3.2 Exclusion Criteria

1. Review articles, editorials, commentaries without original data
2. Qualitative resistance reports without quantitative data
3. Laboratory-selected strains only (no field populations)
4. Novel experimental insecticides not in operational use
5. Control mortality > 20%
6. Sample size < 20 mosquitoes per bioassay
7. Duplicate publications (most comprehensive retained)

### 3.3 Decision Rules for Special Cases

- **Multiple populations**: Each distinct geographic population treated as a separate observation.
- **Multiple bioassays**: Both WHO and CDC results included; bioassay method coded as a variable.
- **Overlapping publications**: Most comprehensive publication retained.
- **Missing sample sizes**: Default n = 100 applied for WHO/CDC bioassays (standard 4 replicates × 25 mosquitoes).
- **Control mortality 5–20%**: Abbott's correction applied.

## 5. Study Selection

Study selection proceeds through two phases:

- **Phase 1 (Title/Abstract)**: Two reviewers independently screen all deduplicated records. Records classified as Include, Exclude, or Uncertain. Uncertain records advance to Phase 2. Discrepancies resolved by discussion.
- **Phase 2 (Full-text)**: Full texts assessed against complete inclusion and exclusion criteria. Exclusion reasons documented using standardized categories. A 20% random sample independently verified by a second reviewer.

## 6. Data Extraction

Data are extracted using a standardized extraction form by one reviewer, with a 20% random sample independently verified by a second reviewer. Discrepancies exceeding 5% trigger complete re-extraction.

## 7. Quality Assessment

Methodological quality is assessed using a modified Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS) adapted for insecticide resistance bioassay studies, evaluating three domains:

- **Selection** (0–4 stars): Representativeness of field population, adequacy of sample size, use of appropriate reference strain, description of collection methodology.
- **Comparability** (0–2 stars): Control of confounding factors (temperature, humidity, mosquito age), adherence to standard WHO or CDC protocols.
- **Outcome assessment** (0–3 stars): Appropriateness of statistical analysis, adequacy of follow-up time, completeness of reporting.

Total quality scores range from 0 to 9, classified as low (0–3), medium (4–6), or high (7–9).

## 8. Effect Size Measures and Transformations

### 8.1 Freeman-Tukey Double Arcsine Transformation (Mortality)

t_i = arcsin(√(x_i/(n_i+1))) + arcsin(√((x_i+1)/(n_i+1)))

v_i = 1/(n_i+1)

where x_i = number dead, n_i = number tested.

### 8.2 Logit Transformation (*kdr* Allele Frequencies)

y_i = ln(p_i/(1−p_i))

v_i = 1/(n_i × p_i) + 1/(n_i × (1−p_i))

where p_i = allele frequency, n_i = number of alleles genotyped. Continuity correction of 0.5 applied when p_i = 0 or p_i = 1.

### 8.3 Natural Log Transformation (Resistance Ratios)

y_i = ln(RR_i)

SE(y_i) = (ln(upper CI_i) − ln(lower CI_i)) / (2 × 1.96)

### 8.4 Standardized Mean Difference (Enzyme Activity)

Hedges' g with small-sample bias correction.

## 9. Meta-Analytical Models

Separate random-effects meta-analyses are conducted for each indicator–insecticide combination using the DerSimonian-Laird method for estimating between-study variance (τ²). Knapp-Hartung adjustment is applied to confidence intervals to account for uncertainty in τ² estimation. Restricted Maximum Likelihood (REML) estimation is performed as a sensitivity analysis.

### 9.1 Heterogeneity Assessment

Heterogeneity is assessed using Cochran's Q statistic, the I² statistic with 95% confidence intervals (interpreted as low <25%, moderate 25–75%, or high >75%), τ² with its standard error, and prediction intervals.

## 10. Cross-Resistance Analysis

Cross-resistance between insecticide classes is quantified using three complementary approaches:

1. **Study-level correlation analysis**: Spearman rank correlation coefficients calculated between resistance indicators for each pair of insecticide classes within studies testing the same population. Pooled using Fisher's z-transformation in a random-effects model.

2. **Population-level co-occurrence analysis**: Odds ratios for co-occurrence of confirmed resistance to each pair of insecticide classes, pooled using the Mantel-Haenszel method with random-effects adjustment.

3. **Mechanism-phenotype linkage**: Multivariate meta-regression with *kdr* allele frequency as a moderator variable for bioassay mortality across different insecticide classes.

## 11. Subgroup Analyses and Meta-Regression

Pre-specified subgroup analyses by: (1) WHO region; (2) time period (before 2010, 2010–2019, 2020–present); (3) insecticide class; (4) mosquito species; (5) life stage; (6) bioassay method; and (7) study quality. Differences assessed using Q test for interaction.

Mixed-effects meta-regression with candidate moderators: collection year, latitude, insecticide class, species, life stage, bioassay method, study quality. Moderators significant at p < 0.10 in univariate models included in multivariate model.

## 12. Publication Bias Assessment

Publication bias assessed through: funnel plots, Egger's weighted regression test, Begg's rank correlation test, and the trim-and-fill method.

## 13. Sensitivity Analyses

1. Leave-one-out analysis
2. Cumulative meta-analysis (chronological)
3. Comparison of variance estimators (DerSimonian-Laird, REML, Paule-Mandel)
4. Influence diagnostics (Cook's distance, DFBETAS)
5. Restriction to high-quality studies (NOS ≥ 7)

## 14. WHO Resistance Classification Criteria

| Mortality (%) | Classification | Interpretation |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| ≥ 98% | Susceptible | Population is susceptible |
| 90–97% | Possible resistance | Resistance suggested, requires confirmation |
| < 90% | Confirmed resistance | Resistance confirmed |
